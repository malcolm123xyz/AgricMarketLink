CONSTITUTION OF OLD STUDENTS OF NAVRONGO

SENIOR HIGH SCHOOL OF 2004 BATCH

(NABIA 04)

 

 

JUNE, 2018

 

 

 

 

 

 

PREAMBLE  

We, the old students of Navrongo Senior High School of 2004 batch hereinafter referred to as NABIA 04, 

CONVINCED that the greatness of a school depends on the concerted efforts of both past and present students, members of staff, parents and guardians BELIEVING sincerely that a Union of Old Students is an important instrument for the growth and development of a school 

DESIRING to form bonds of friendship and unity among ourselves 

REALIZING that without an effective organization of ourselves our aims and objectives cannot be achieved 

WISHING to create avenues through which we can offer ourselves the benefits of a united front necessary for service to OURSELVES, SCHOOL and NATION 

DETERMINED to ensure proper management of our union 

DO HEREBY PROPOSE the PROMULGATION and ADOPTION of this CONSTITUTION to ourselves, on this 25TH JUNE, 2018, as the primary document governing all and every activity of our union.

ARTICLE 1: NAME  

  • The Union shall be known as “old students of Navrongo Senior Secondary School of 2004 batch” (Old Navascans Union) and its members shall be referred to as “NABIA 04”.
  • The Union shall be duly registered under the mother body-NABIA National.
  • The Union shall be duly registered as a Charity.

ARTICLE 2: AIMS AND OBJECTIVES  

The following shall be the aims and objectives of the Union;

  • To promote understanding and unity among NABIA 04.
  • To render the necessary moral, material and welfare support to NABIA

04 as may be necessary.

  • To provide networking among stakeholders of the School
  • There shall be established a NABIA 04 Welfare Fund (NAWELF) which shall cover the welfare matters of the NABIA 04 and support the wider ambition of educational advancement of Navrongo Senior secondary School and the country as a whole.
  • The NAWELF, referred to in Clause 4 of Article 2, shall be governed by rules and regulations made for the purpose by NABIA 04.
  • To act in concert with unions of like nature in Ghana in order to stimulate and sustain the socio-economic and educational advancement of Navrongo Senior secondary School and the country as a whole.

ARTICLE 3: MEMBERSHIP

  • Membership shall be open to any person who has been a student and have successfully completed the school in 2004, having been assigned a Folio Number.
  • Notwithstanding clause (1) of this Article the General Meeting of NABIA 04 may grant any person Associate membership of NABIA 04.
  • A member shall pay a membership registration which shall be set from time to time by the General Meeting of NABIA 04.
  • A member shall pay a monthly dues which shall be set from time to time by the General Meeting of NABIA 04.
  • The General Meeting of NABIA 04 shall, subject to the rules of natural justice, have the power to suspend or expel a member who betrays the cause of

NABIA 04 or who brings the name of NABIA 04 into disrepute. 

ARTICLE 4: THE NABIA 04 EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

  • There shall be a NABIA 04 Executive Committee, herein referred to as the NEC.
  • The NEC shall consist of the President, Vice-President, General Secretary, Assistant General Secretary, Organiser, Treasurer, Assistant Treasurer, Women’s Commissioner, NAWELF Officer, Public Relations Officer (PRO), and Legal Adviser.
  • The NEC may co-opt any NABIA 04 into the NEC, provided that the coopted members are not more than one-third of the NEC.
  • The NEC shall meet at least twice in a year.
  • The NEC shall convene General Meetings of NABIA 04.

ARTICLE 5: DUTIES OF OFFICERS  

1) THE PRESIDENT 

The President shall be the head of the NEC and shall preside over all meetings of NABIA 04. 

2) THE VICE PRESIDENT 

The Vice-President shall perform the duties of the President in the absence of the President and any other duties the President may assign or delegate to the Vice President.

3) THE GENERAL SECRETARY  

  1. The General Secretary shall be responsible for the preparation and presentation of minutes of meetings of the NEC, and the General Meetings of NABIA 04.
  2. The General Secretary shall be responsible for all the correspondence of

NABIA 04 and shall keep records relating there to. iii. The General Secretary shall perform any other duties that may be assigned by the NEC, or General Meeting of NABIA 04. 

4) THE ASSISTANT GENERAL SECRETARY  

The Assistant General Secretary shall perform the duties of the General Secretary in the absence of the General Secretary and any other duties delegated by the General Secretary. 

5) THE NABIA 04 TREASURER

  1. The Treasurer shall be responsible for overseeing the administration of NABIA 04 Accounts and shall ensure proper accountability for such

funds. 

  1. The Treasurer shall be responsible for keeping the funds of NABIA 04 and shall see to the proper accounting for the said of such funds.
  • The Treasurer shall submit a report on the finances of NABIA 04 to the

NEC and to the General Meeting of NABIA 04. 

6) THE NABIA 04 ASSISTANT TREASURER  

The Assistant Treasurer shall perform the duties of the Treasurer in the absence of the Treasurer and any other duties delegated by the Treasurer to the Assistant Treasurer.

6) THE NABIA 04 ORGANISER

The Organiser shall be responsible for organising all NABIA 04 national events

7) THE NABIA 04 WOMEN’S COMMISSIONER

  1. The women’s commissioner shall serve as a liaison officer in the affairs of NABIA 04 women.
  2. The Women’s commissioner shall also serve as a representative of NABIA 04 women at the National NABIA Women Association.

8) NABIA 04 WELFARE FUND OFFICER

  1. There shall be a NABIA 04 Welfare Fund Officer who shall be in-charge of the day to day administration of the NAWELF created under Article 2 of this constitution.
  2. The NAWELF Officer shall be in charge of the day to day administration of the NAWELF as specified in the NAWELF Rules and Regulations.

9) PUBLIC RELATIONS OFFICER (PRO)

The PRO shall be responsible for broadcasting all events of NABIA 04

10) LEGAL ADVISOR

The legal advisor shall be responsible for the provision of legal advices to matters of NABIA 04.

ARTICLE 6: ELECTION OF OFFICERS

  • Only NABIA 04 in good standing, at a meeting, shall have the right to vote and be voted for.
  • There shall be no voting by proxy.
  • Election to offices under this constitution shall be held in physical or Virtual mediums and decisions shall be by a simple majority of the votes cast. 4) A member, qualified to be elected to any office under the constitution shall be nominated by one other qualified member and seconded by at least two

(2) other qualified members in good standing. 

  • All elections shall be conducted at General Meetings.
  • The General Meeting shall appoint a three-member election committee to conduct the elections in accordance with provisions of this constitution provided that the election of officers immediately before the coming into force of this constitution shall be conducted by the constitutional review committee.

ARTICLE 7: NABIA 04 WELFARE FUND & DUES

  • Members shall be levied an initial monthly dues of GH¢5 (GH¢60.00 per annum) to cover matters of NAWELF and support in running NABIA 04.
  • Membership dues shall be set from time to time by the General Meeting of NABIA 04.
  • Sixty percent (60%) of membership dues shall be used for the established NAWELF, as referred to in clause 4 of the article 2.
  • Forty percent (40%) of membership dues shall be used for running the NABIA 04 Association.
  • Only NABIA 04 of good standing (i.e. members who have duly paid their dues) would benefit from this NAWELF.
  • Matters of Welfare to be considered for NABIA 04 shall include; funeral of parent or spouse, wedding, and any special request need (s) that is (are) duly assessed and accepted by NABIA 04 in a general meeting.

ARTICLE 8: TENURE OF OFFICE  

  • Subject to Clause (2) of this Article, a member of the NEC shall hold office for a term of two years and shall be eligible for re-election for a further term of two years only.
  • Without prejudice to Clause (1) of this Article, a member of the NEC may be removed from office in the following manner:
    1. A vote of no confidence may be passed at a General Meeting by twothirds of members present and voting.
    2. Notice of the vote of no confidence shall be served together with the notice of the General Meeting as an agenda item.
  • The grounds of the vote of no confidence shall be stated in the notice.
  1. The affected officer or officers shall be afforded the opportunity to defend themselves.
  • Any member of the NEC may resign by a letter addressed to the NEC.
  • There shall be a by-election whenever a vacancy occurs in the membership of the NEC.

ARTICLE 9: COMMITTEES.  

1) The General Meeting may establish ad-hoc committees for the purposes of achieving any or all of the objectives stated in Article 2 of this Constitution, or to perform any specific duties in the interest of NABIA 04. 

ARTICLE 10: GENERAL MEETING:

  • The General Meeting of NABIA 04 shall be the highest decision-making body of NABIA 04.
  • The General Meeting shall be held at least once every year.
  • An Emergency General Meeting may be called by the NEC and such emergency meetings shall have the same authority as the General Meeting.
  • The General meeting of Nabia 04 shall be held through Physical or Virtual mediums
  • The quorum of any General Meeting shall not be less than one-half of Nabia 04 in good standing.
  • Any Nabia 04, in good standing, who wants any matter to be on the agenda of the General Meeting shall notify the General Secretary no later than 30 days before the opening of the General Meeting at which meeting the matter may be considered.

ARTICLE 11: FINANCE AND INCOME  

  • The sources of finance and income of NABIA 04 shall include:
    1. Monthly dues as may be determined from time to time by the General Meeting.
    2. Levies and contributions as may be imposed from time to time by the General Meeting.
  • Donations, fund raising and sponsorship
  1. Any other source of finance
  • A member shall pay dues, levies, fees and contributions as determined by a resolution of the General Meeting from time to time.

ARTICLE 12: BANK ACCOUNT(S)  

  • NABIA 04 shall have a Bank Account or Bank Accounts with a Bank or Banks as the General Meeting may determine.
  • All funds due NABIA 04 shall be paid into a NABIA 04 Bank Account /

Accounts approved by the General Meeting. 

  • There shall be three signatories to NABIA 04 Bank Account(s).
  • The three signatories to operate NABIA 04 Bank Account(s) shall be:
    1. The President
    2. The Treasurer
  • The General Secretary.
  • The President and National Treasurer shall be the principal signatories for any transaction in respect of NABIA 04 Bank Account(s).
  • The General Secretary shall sign in place of the Treasurer in the absence of the Treasurer.

ARTICLE 13: AUDIT  

  • All NABIA 04 accounts shall be subject to annual audit by a reputable firm in accordance with national law.
  • Audited NABIA 04 Accounts shall be made available at the General Meeting.

ARTICLE 14: AMMENDMENTS  

  • No person shall arrogate any authority to oneself which does not emanate from this constitution.
  • Any amendments to this Constitution can only be made by NABIA 04 at a General Meeting.
  • A General Meeting of NABIA 04 duly convened for amendments, among others, may amend or abrogate this Constitution in the following manner:
  1. By proposal for amendment, made in writing, to the General Secretary at least thirty (30) days before a General Meeting at which such proposal is to be considered. ii. A submission for amendment to this constitution shall be adopted by one-third of members in good standing.
